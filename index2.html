Unfortunately Africa also has the highest health burden in the world. Diseases such as malaria, tuberculosis, and HIV/AIDS are prevalent and the high cost of diagnosis and treatment is an obstacle towards combating these problems.

Mobile phones are a major source of communication and means of taking information and technology to rural and remote areas of the continent. With low-cost and readily available mobile phones, underprivileged, low-income earners and rural dwellers can also participate in the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) revolution.


This means that changing how health information and services are delivered using eHealth solutions can have significant impact.




Our mission is to improve healthcare access by connecting patients to actionable health information and in doing so enhancing the interaction between patients and their doctor.


We want to do this by providing a platform to improve the patient-doctor relationship by ensuring that primary care to the patient extends past the initial doctor checkup.

We are currently planning to launch our service beginning in Cameroon later this year.
